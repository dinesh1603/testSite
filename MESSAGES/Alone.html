'OBSERVE ORDER, AS all creation does! Arrogance is absurd, for man is nothing! Without love, nothing is acceptable to God. Christ showed us that nothing is more beautiful than this when He gave His own Flesh for our flesh and His Soul for our souls.

'We must hasten with all earnestness and readiness of mind, to perfect every good work, labouring with cheerfulness; for even the Creator and LORD of all things rejoices in His own works'

- This was the voice of one Jewish convert, considered one of the disciples of Peter and Paul, the Apostles. His name was Clement. Tradition has it that he was the same Clement who was mentioned in Philippians 4:3 as 'fellow labourer'.

 

IN THE YEAR 96 AD, as per the history, Clement wrote a letter to the Corinthians. Because in the Corinthian Church, a violent faction once again erupted vehemently and ruined the Church meticulously. Earlier, Paul also in Chapter 3 of his first Epistle to this Church, severely warned such groups in his carefully chosen words.

Even so, these warring groups did not desist themselves from their carnality by envying divisions. So only it was necessary for clement, being a 'fellow labourer' of Paul, to make an appeal to their discipline within the Church and to overcome the jealousy which had been the cause for their internal strife.

But in 100 AD, Clement was exiled to Crimea, by the persecuting emperor, Trajan. In his exile, Clement became the consolation for some 2,000 fellow Christian prisoners. During that time, he once said: 'God has granted me a grace, of which, I was not worthy, by sending me amongst you, to share your crown.' Such was his passion for His Church and also for suffering for His sake.

He never wanted to be a great man or a leader or a master in his life; but, his entire nature was: LET HIM ALONE...!

What about you and me?

Do the others see our LORD, the Christ in us or do they see only our personality in ourselves?
